Adding Decs to milPDS



Decs are often received via mail or email and we manually add them to milPDS when they were not processed through vPC.



Steps to adding decs on milPDS:
  1. Go to milPDS: Press Ctrl+L, then type in %award and Awards and Decorations should pop up
  2. Find the member: Press the [F11] on keyboard. Enter the SSN with dashes, then Ctrl+[F11]. Columns will populate with the member’s information. Click on member’s name.
    **If you don't have social, you can also find the member with the % sign at the end.
    Example: To look for "SNUFFY, BILLY", type something like "SNUFFY, B%" in the name box.
  3. Here, you'll see member's AWARDS and DECORATIONS where you will see a list of awards/decs that the member already has.
    Refer to the Awards and Decs Listing button above and check the list to see what your award/decoration.
    **Use Ctrl + F and type in the award/dec for the milPDS code you are looking for in the list
  4. Verify that the award/dec is already made in milPDS.
    If not, then you'll click the tiny blank space under Details (highlighted and labelled it 1 in picture below) and then click the icon at the top left (highlighted and labelled 2 in the picture below)


    For AWARDS, it will look like this:


    while for Decorations it looks like this:


    Further explanation on the Decoration input:
    action: ADD to add a new decoration to the member's profile
    dec: refer to reference for decorations to find milpds code from the button above
    num: is how many of the same medal person has (so if first one, its 1)
    order num: look at "special order' at bottom of the award, then only type in the numbers
    order date: the "given under my hand date"
    **skip headquarter
    start n close date is the one above "accomplishments"
    app/disapprov. date is same as order date
    **leave app/dis. level blank
    condition is at the bottom - condition matters because if they're retiring/pcsing we need to know what its for
    grade is the dude's rank
    pas - at the bottom but if just yyyy, skip
    service component: always R (active duty)
  5. Then Save.

Other miscellanous things regarding decorations

What are devices??



A device is an addition to the Award or ribbon that the member is given.

What are Oak Leaf Clusters??



You'll notice that some Decorations come with things like 1OLC or 2OLC. "oak leaf clusters" means its not the first time the member got that specific medal.

Criteria to add ribbons to member's vMPF/milPDS profile



Often we will get emails from customers and CSS stating that their ribbons are missing, or they were awarded a ribbon or decoration and need to get it added to their profile.
In order to update the profiles, we need verifications from the members or their CSS showing that they qualify for the awards/decs.

Frequently asked questions:

Customer's question: Hi, I got deployed overseas and qualified for a short tour ribbon. What do I need to provide to get that updated in my profile?
What to do: The customer will need to provide any documentation that shows that they were overseas for 300+ days within an 18-month time span.
It could be any of the following documentations:
orders
travel voucher
SURF
another decoration or award recommendation
etcetera
For a long tour ribbon, the member must have been oversease for 365+ days within an 3-year time span.


Customer: Hi, I got deployed overseas and qualified for a long tour ribbon. What do I need to provide to get that updated in my profile?
You will need to provide any documentation that shows you were overseas . It could be your orders, travel voucher, your SURF.

Decs in the mail



When we receive decs in the mail, we usually get 2 extra (printed) copies. Update to milpds using steps above, keep 1 copy then mail out other copy to AFPC (if no extra copy just scan it then toss into tab).
Our copy we scan into share drive.
Then put physical copy into the binder
The mail out copy in the correct drawer tab under the printer area (officer vs enlisted)
*we send to AFPC so they can update prda, takes 4-6 weeks to reflect on prda


If a mailed dec comes in with a "transittal of decorations" then scan both the dec and the transmittal to put into shared drive, then you can shred it
